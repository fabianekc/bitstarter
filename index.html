The internet enables corporations to gather huge amounts of everyone's data to tailor ads, news, and search results. This is commonly referred to as 'Big Data' and was for some time only accessible to large companies like Google, Amazon, or Facebook. However, it is your right to access and use this data for your own benefit!

Hyve.me challenges todays assumption that it requires a group of experts and lots of money to make sense out of data. We have broken down the process of data analysis and tackle now all questions relevant for individuals using Big Data.

Think of Hyve.me as what the Linux community did to operating systems. 20 years ago there were only a few major companys who covered the market with proprietary software. With Hyve.me we want to create the same open ecosystem for data as Linux did for software: Everybody should have access to his or her data and process it in whatever way desired. There should be a free market and open competition between the best algorithms and visualization methods - and Hyve.me will provide the infrastructure.

However, to succeed we need to overcome the network effect: as more users join the service the more benefits it generates for the overall community!

Please spread the word about Hyve.me by sharing the information on your preferred social forum, and sign-up on Hyve.me to share your own projects.

FAQ

There are a number of challenges to deal with when working on Big Data for individuals and here are answers to some of the questions you might have:

How do you optimize projects and identify relevant data?
Hyve.me automatically shows similar projects based on your project categorization. Learning from metrics used by others you can also get a new perspective on your endeavour (we call this "from vanity metrics to valuable insight"). In future versions it will also be possible to compare data in a secure and anonymous way.
Where does the relevant data come from and how to get it?
It is the goal to automatically import all your data that is stored by 3rd parties about you. Currently, only manual entry and data collection via email is available.
Where and how to store data securly?
For now, all your data is stored using Amazon Web Services. In the near future you will be able to choose your storage provider. It is also planned to provide the option for storing your data only on a device you trust (e.g., your PC at home) and all processing will be performed locally.
Who can see and access data?
Every user will be able to define who can access what data. Right now the default level is defined that everybody can see what you are tracking (i.e., the attributes) but not the data itself.
How to aggregate data from users while maintaining full privacy?
Hyve.me proposes an architecture and a protocol for secure and anonymous data exchange between users. This will enable every user to run statistical analysis without disclosing his or her own data. This is the main area of development right now.
How to process the data?
While Hyve.me will provide a basic set of analysis tools, it will also provide an open market for algorithms. There, data analysis experts can upload various implementations for specific fields, and users can choose from the available offerings.
How to interprete raw data and results of processed data?
As with algorithms there is a basic set of graphical representation of your data available on Hyve.me. For more sophisticated visualizations an open market will bring together visualization experts and users.
How to assess the value of data in case you want to buy, sell, or trade data?
Eventually, it is the goal of Hyve.me to provide a platform for trading data. Even if you don't plan to sell some of your data, this is the place to find the market value of the information you possess.
